📅 How Spaced Repetition Works

FSRS is a modern spacing algorithm superior to legacy SuperMemo (SM-2) systems. It models your memory decay and customizes intervals based on how you grade your performance.

Grade Your Recall

Again

Complete blackout, or you couldn't identify the pattern. The card is scheduled for immediate re-learning.

Hard

You remembered the pattern with extreme effort or made minor mistakes. Spacing interval increases slightly.

Good

Standard recall. You remembered the solution approach in reasonable time. Interval is expanded optimally.

Easy

You knew the solution instantly. The pattern is too trivial. Spacing interval is set far in the future.

⚙️ FSRS Customizer Guide & Data

Understand the math behind the scheduler and configure FSRS parameters to match your learning speed.

💡 Understanding FSRS Parameters

FSRS (Free Spaced Repetition Scheduler) uses target recall rate and stabilizing coefficients to scale review intervals dynamically:

  • Target Memory Retention Rate: The target probability that you will successfully recall the coding pattern. Setting a higher rate (e.g., 95%) yields shorter, more frequent intervals. Setting a lower rate (e.g., 80%) yields longer spacing between reviews. Recommended range: 0.75 - 0.95.
  • Decay Constant (-0.5): Represents how quickly retrievability decays exponentially over elapsed time. Standard FSRS optimization anchors this at -0.5.
  • Factor Constant (19/81 ≈ 0.2346): Scales stability calculations.

📊 Coefficient Groups (w0 - w16)

FSRS coefficients scale stability and difficulty updates based on your recall rating:

  • Initial Stability (w0 - w3): Spacing values in days for rating Again, Hard, Good, Easy on your first review.
  • Initial Difficulty (w4 - w5): Base difficulty level and rating multiplier.
  • Stability Boosts (w6 - w10): Rate of stability increase for correct answers.
  • Lapse Modifiers (w11 - w14): Deceleration coefficient when you fail a card.
  • Easy Bonus (w15 - w16): Additional scheduling bonus and stability bounds.

🏷️ Tag-Specific Profiles

Have custom weights profiles for specific topics! You can assign dedicated sets of 17 coefficients to specific tags (e.g. Graphs or Sliding Window):

  1. Open FSRS Customizer from the popup dashboard.
  2. Scroll to Tag-Specific Profiles at the bottom.
  3. Enter the tag name (e.g. Dynamic Programming) and paste the 17 coefficients.
  4. Click Add Profile. Coding cards matching this tag will dynamically use these coefficients.
